Opening the preview.

This build is ad-hoc signed, not notarized. macOS will be dramatic about it once. That's expected for now.

  1. 01Download and unzip WhisperQuiet.zip, then drag WhisperQuiet into Applications.
  2. 02macOS 15 (Sequoia) or newer: open the app once, macOS will block it, then go to System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll to the "WhisperQuiet was blocked" notice, click Open Anyway, and confirm (you may be asked to authenticate). Open the app again after that.
  3. 03macOS 13-14: right-click the app, choose Open, then choose Open again.
  4. 04Grant Microphone, Accessibility, and Input Monitoring. Quit and reopen once after granting permissions.
  5. 05Hold right Option, speak, release. If something breaks, that feedback is useful.

The point is local.

Cloud dictation tools proved people want fast speech-to-text everywhere. The annoying part is paying a monthly bill for something a modern Mac can already run privately.

Cloud dictation

  • Audio has to leave your machine before text comes back.
  • Useful features get bundled into another subscription.
  • Your network quality becomes part of your writing tool.

WhisperQuiet

  • Works in whatever already has focus: Mail, Slack, Notes, a browser field.
  • Speech runs on-device after the one-time model download.
  • No account, usage meter, or transcription dashboard.
  • 2.0% word error rate on quiet speech, 7.1% whispered.Internal benchmark, measured on-device.

By default, WhisperQuiet keeps each recording's audio (capped at 512 MB, 30 days) and its transcript on-device to improve accuracy over time. Nothing leaves your Mac, and either can be turned off in config.
